Therapy Services & Specialities

What I Help With

Trauma
  • Whether you've experienced a single traumatic event or have survived complex trauma, the goal of trauma work is to put you in control of your life's story. We'll work at a pace that prioritizes your safety as we embark on this deep work to process your past, honor your resilience, release emotions and sensations, and forge a new path forward.
  • Trauma has a tendency of compounding and manifesting other emotional challenges when your pain goes without being acknowledged or supported. Whether your trauma shows up as changes in mood such as depression or anxiety, substance use, self-harm, or chronic suicidal thoughts, I'm here to provide you with a place to address these challenges without fear of judgment and inappropriate hospitalization. Together, we'll develop a safety plan that centers your voice, build your access to resources for reducing harm, and add new coping tools to your regulation toolbox.
Self-Esteem
  • You deserve to recognize your own self-worth. We'll reveal and harness the strengths you intrinsically have and celebrate you through exercises of self-compassion, respect, and gratitude. This work is intended to be gentle and yielding while also nudging you to confront stuck narratives, relationships, and traumas that hold you back from the confidence you desire.
Identity & Purpose
  • Answering the questions “who am I” or “what's the point” is intimidating, especially if you've been taught that who you are is wrong, too much, or not enough. We'll take small steps to delve into the many parts of you, uncover masked facets of your identity, and venture forth into new experiences to develop a life that aligns with the whole you.
Neurodiversity
  • Cognitive, behavioral, or exposure-based therapies aren't always developed with your type of neurodiversity in mind. By using neuroaffirming therapy approaches designed to work with your brain, we can navigate burnout, sensory sensitivities, regulation and accommodation needs, cross-neurotype communication, ableism, and self-acceptance.
LGBTQIA2S+
  • If you've ever felt the frustration of having to explain your relationship or pronouns to your therapist, I see you. No matter where you are in your journey, our space is one where your true self can be explored, expressed, and embraced. As we navigate the fears and systemic blockades to you living authentically, we will empower you to grow your community and foster stronger connections between your mind, spirit, and body.
Sexuality
  • Sex is a language you use to connect with other people, express yourself, experience pleasure, and try new things. In our work together, we will label and deconstruct the physical, emotional, and systemic obstacles you face to pave the way to more enjoyable, safe sex.

How I Help

You exist as a product of the many systems in which you are an important part. I employ a systemic approach to therapy to acknowledge how your family, community, government, culture, etc. impacts how you think, feel, and show up in the world. I also operate on a person-centered modality because therapy works best when it's tailored to your specific strengths and needs. As a radically affirming therapist, my therapy space incorporates harm-reduction, feminist, critical race, LGBTQIA2S+-affirming, and neurodivergent-affirming frameworks.

Therapy can and should be built with you in mind. When we discuss your goals, you and I will work together on a treatment plan that considers your needs as well as your comfort level. For holistic trauma treatment, I blend elements of various therapies to recruit and strengthen your mind, body, and spiritual connections. Some types of therapy we might use include:

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR)

Somatic (Body-centered) Therapy

Internal Family Systems (Parts Work)

Ecotherapy (Nature-based Therapy)

Mindfulness-Based Therapy

What to Expect

Sessions with me start off with a brief check-in (“how's work been?", "how was that concert?”, “did you paint your room like you planned?”, etc.). After, we'll set the intention for our session or if we're already planning to work on something, we'll revisit it and then jump in.

Whether you choose to attend your session virtually from the comfort of your own home, or in person with me in my cozy Wethersfield, CT office, your session is your space to make your own! I always encourage you do what you need to centered-- pop in with your favorite beverage, draw or write, play some music, or get yourself a little treat. In my office, I always have games, drinks, snacks, fidgets, and a plush BLÅHAJ to make you feel grounded while we work together.

FAQs

What are your standard rates for therapy services?
  • Intake session (60 minutes+): $200
  • 60 minute individual session: $130
  • 60 minute family or partners session: $170
  • Extended sessions of 75, 90, or 120 minutes may be available for an additional fee.
  • Sliding scale and payment options are usually available.
Do you offer chemistry calls?
  • Yes! I offer free 15-minute chemistry or consultation calls to talk about your goals, what you're looking for in a therapist, and see if we'd be a good match. Click here to book your consultation!
Do you accept insurance?
  • At this time, I do not accept insurance, but I provide super-bills through Sessions Health to make insurance reimbursement easier for folx with out-of-network plan coverage. I also offer a select number of sliding scale sessions weekly for folx who have more limited income to cover necessities. Please contact me for more information or to check availability!
How do I pay for services?
  • I accept Venmo, PayPal, all major credit or debit cards via Stripe, and HSA and FSA!
What is your cancellation policy?
  • I request cancellations are made at least 24 hours in advance. I forgive first-time late-cancellations or missed sessions, and each subsequent cancellation is subject to the total session fee. When availability permits, I waive the cancellation fee if the session is rescheduled within the same calendar week.
Are your sessions in-person or virtual?
  • I currently offer both in-person sessions at my office in Wethersfield, CT and virtual sessions to all residents located within the states of Connecticut or Massachusetts. I utilize HIPAA-compliant virtual health platforms for all client sessions and documentation, such as the electronic health record system, Sessions Health, in tandem with Google Meets.
  • I provide individual ecotherapy sessions in-person in a mutually agreed-upon location. These are made available by request April through October when weather and season permits for folx at state and municipal parks located in the Central Connecticut region (Hartford, New Haven, Middlesex, and Tolland counties).
  • I do not provide in-person services at this time.
What are your office hours?
  • My typical office hours are Mondays, Tuesdays, Wednesdays, and Fridays from 8:00am to 8:00pm EST, and closed for weekends and major holidays.
